Maulin Hemani

Maulin serves as a co-founder of Diagraphic Technologies. He is currently a Freshman at Northwestern's McCormick School of Engineering. Maulin is an experienced entrepreneur and leader. He co-founded a non-profit organization and has served as the head of the Pingry Blue Key Society and the Pingry Speech team. At Diagraphic Technologies, Maulin is the head of business operations and company vision. Chalk, the first product for Diagraphic Technologies, was originally imagined by Maulin, and he hopes to continue expanding Diagraphic Technologies as well as identifying new markets to develop platforms for.

Amol Kapoor

Amol is a co-founder of Diagraphic Technologies and currently a Freshman at Columbia's School of Engineering. Amol has been coding since he was 11 years old and has held various leadership positions, including head of his high school Debate and Independent Research teams. Amol brings both technological and managerial expertise to Diagraphic Technologies and serves as the head of technical development. As a core developer for both Chalk and Scribe, Amol has also worked closely with school technology committees to introduce the program to students and teachers alike. He plans to continue developing new products and software for Diagraphic Technologies.

Eric Klawitter

Eric is a software developer at Diagraphic Tech working to develop features for Scribe on both the front end and back end. He started coding four years ago and since then has served as the co-leader of his school's Student Technology Committee. Eric is currently a freshman at Georgia Tech studying computer engineering. His work at Diagraphic Technologies is centered around streamlining the process of annotating a presentation to enable more effective use of slideshows.

Derek Hong

Derek is a software developer at Diagraphic Technologies working on the functionality of Scribe. He is currently 18 years old and a Freshman at Brown University. Though Derek's coding experience only started in 10th grade, he hopes to further his education at Brown and continue working on projects at Diagraphic Tech. In Scribe, Derek sees a new realm of potential for real-time, teacher-student interaction. He hopes that Diagraphic Technologies will continue to integrate teaching and technology and that he can continue to create platforms that process along.

Adrian Garza

Adrian is a Columbia University sophomore who brings his creative mind and artistic sensibilities to Diagraphic Tech. He started coding in college, and is currently improving his simulation of an alien ecosystem on the planet Jupiter. He writes for the satirical newspaper the Federalist and makes bad art in his spare time.

Alex Epifano

Alex serves as sysadmin and manages the Diagraphic Technologies webstack. Having programmed for more than half of his life, he continues his pursuit of knowledge as a rising sophomore at Georgia Tech. He has managed servers both at a large web-based company and on his own for personal projects. Alex brings this knowledge as well as a passion for highly performant web systems to Diagraphic Technologies.
